Echo of a Dream

I had a dream last night.
It felt too real—
two people, no fights,
just love, still whole.

They said distance breaks some,
but not the ones who hold on,
who stay loyal across oceans,
who believe in the unseen.

A warmth lingered in that dream—
a memory, maybe.
Of hugs that melted the world outside.
Of eyes that once teared up over little things.

But then came the echo.
Not of betrayal,
but of choices.
Of two people standing still,
but on different roads.

I woke up with a weight in my chest.
A quiet ache that words can’t fix.
Sometimes the hardest part
is knowing what could’ve been fixed
with just a little more belief.

If ever she reads the winds of this world,
may she find peace,
and walk paths that don’t need
to prove or pretend.

This isn’t a story of blame,
just of what’s left behind
when hearts part with loose ends
and too much hope.
